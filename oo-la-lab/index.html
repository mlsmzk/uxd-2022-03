Project Proposal

Receipt Tracking for People, centered on Freelancers and Content Creators

What is the problem in brief?


Individuals who are self-employed or work in industries that are not formally recognized have a hard time understanding, tracking, and filing their taxes. Most of the time these individuals are creatives and do not have any tools to understand what tax laws and brackets apply to them. Moreover, they do not take advantage of the specific tax returns or write offs that may be available to them. And even if they are aware of the system, they need to keep track of their daily expenses and understand how to divide personal expenses from business expenses. This is often done right before the tax deadlines, where you submit all the receipts you can get your hands on to a tax consultant and wait for them to figure out what your final tax amount will be. However, many creatives/freelancers who are starting out do not have the financial means to have an inhouse tax consultant. Apart from the logistical issues, there is also a primary behavioral issue where individuals focused on creative work overlook their financial obligations. Building an app catered specifically to this persona will provide them with an easy method to fulfill those responsibilities and, eventually, modify their behavior.

How will your project change the status quo? For whom?


Our goal is that our product can help ease the process of filing taxes for individuals who would otherwise struggle and need to pay an external person to do them. Moreover, it will establish a sense of ownership and responsibility as individuals learn to manage their finances and leverage tax systems. Also, in doing so, we increase our own general knowledge of filing taxes on a personal and a micro scale.

What is (at least) one naïve design idea (rough sketch)? How does this idea address what you see as the core problem?


We think the core problem is not knowing how your expenses and income look like over time, and how that reflects onto your taxes. Our design above aims to fix that with easy to view solutions through graphs and easily digestible boxes of information. However, we decided to not add text to these boxes because we are not part of our users, so we hope to talk to them and figure out what information they would want to be displayed here.

What information do you need to collect? How will you collect it? How will you meet your target users, or who can serve as effective proxies for them?


Questions:

  1. What users need this?
    • Self-Employed / Freelancers
    • Not Financially Aware / Inexperienced
    • Cannot afford to hire an accountant
  2. Why do users need this?
    • Legally required to file taxes
    • Taxes for the first time can be confusing to your average person
    • Even experienced tax-doers can ease the process with this tool and perhaps learn something new
  3. How will we collect information
    • We will visit Greentown Labs to interview startup groups. (Ideally 10)
  4. What do these users want out of our service
    • A way to files taxes easily and accurately
    • Somwhere users can keep track of their finances(revenue, income, spending, etc.)

Maybes:

  • What legal counsels/details should be taken into account for the actual implementation. (Possibly out of scope, but useful for context)

How will your team work together to accomplish these goals (as well as your individual learning goals)?


As a design team, we plan to do most of our work in person as a team. We plan on collaborating mostly in the class time we have, but we also set up a when2meet to meet at least once a week outside of class. We agreed to meet every Sunday at 11am to get brunch and get some work done together.

Although it is not our preferred way of working, we also set up a chat on the UXD Discord for online collaboration and general chats for any progress updates we need to be aware of and complete
